## The Struggle with Local Broadcasters

In some regions, including Japan, F1 broadcasting rights have moved to platforms that are either prohibitively expensive or force you to subscribe to a bundle of unrelated content. For fans who only want to watch the qualifying sessions and the races, paying a high monthly fee for a massive library of movies or shows you didn't ask for is a tough pill to swallow.

## F1 TV Pro via VPN is Getting Harder

Up until a few years ago, many fans used VPNs to subscribe to F1 TV Pro in regions like the US. However, F1 has become much stricter. Even with a VPN, many are now blocked because their credit card was not issued in the target country.

I previously found a workaround by subscribing through Google Play while connected to a European VPN, which bypassed the credit card region lock. This allowed me to use the incredible [Multi Viewer](https://multiviewer.app/) app to watch races with live telemetry and driver trackers. Unfortunately, many of these subscription loopholes were closed last year, and even Apple TV integrations which started this year for the US region are now aggressively detecting and blocking VPN users.

## The Solution: Free-to-Air Broadcasters (via VPN)

After some digging on Reddit, I found a reliable way to watch races legally and for free (excluding the cost of a VPN).

In Austria 🇦🇹, two broadcasters—ServusTV and ORF—share the rights and broadcast almost every race live. For example, the Australian GP might be on Servus, while the Chinese GP is on ORF. As long as you are connected to an Austrian VPN server, you can stream these directly from their official websites for free. ORF1 is the main channel to look for.

Alternatively, you can connect to a Belgian 🇧🇪 VPN and use the "Auvio" platform by RTBF. They also broadcast F1 for free and often cover other motorsports like MotoGP as well.

### A Few Notes on This Setup

The footage is the same high-quality feed you'd see on F1 TV, but the commentary will be in German (Austria) or French (Belgium).

Personally, I don't mind this. I usually mute the official commentary and listen to English-speaking F1 YouTubers who do live watch-alongs. It’s often more passionate and insightful than the local TV announcers anyway. Combined with real-time updates from F1 journalists on Bluesky, you get a very rich experience.

**Note:** This works perfectly in a desktop browser, but the official mobile apps for these broadcasters often have more aggressive VPN detection.
